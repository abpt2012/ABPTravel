Divisions

Myanmar is made up of seven divisions, which include many of the country’s major cities and important areas for travel and daily life. These regions feature a mix of busy urban centres, historic sites, and local communities, offering a broader look at how people live across Myanmar.

All Divisions

Ayeyarwady

Ayeyarwady Region is known for its rivers and relaxed coastal areas with a slow, laid-back feel. Ngwe Saung Beach and Chaungtha Beach are popular for easy beach trips and peaceful seaside views.

Bago

Bago Region is a historically rich area filled with impressive pagodas and ancient cities just a short trip from Yangon. Shwemawdaw Pagoda is the real highlight, known as one of the tallest pagodas in the country.

Magway

Magway Region offers dry landscapes, quiet towns, and a peaceful local feel along the Ayeyarwady River. Myathalun Pagoda is its most famous landmark, while Minbu is known for its calm riverside daily life.

Mandalay

Mandalay Region is the cultural heart of Myanmar, rich in history, tradition, and famous landmarks. Mandalay Palace and Bagan are known for their ancient temples, especially the thousands of pagodas across Bagan, and its iconic sunrise views.

Sagaing

Sagaing Region is known for its calm, spiritual feel and river views along the Ayeyarwady. The Sagaing Hills are filled with pagodas and monasteries, while nearby city Monywa is known for its striking Buddha statues.

Tanintharyi

Tanintharyi Region offers long coastlines, tropical islands, and rich marine life with clear waters and soft sandy beaches. The Myeik Archipelago and Dawei are known for beaches and quiet seaside views.

Yangon

Yangon Region is the busiest part of Myanmar, blending colonial buildings with modern city life and busy streets. Shwedagon Pagoda and Sule Pagoda stand at the centre of its culture and history.

Ayeyarwady

A More Detailed Summary of Ayeyarwady

Located in the south of Myanmar, Ayeyarwady Region is known for its wide river landscapes, coastal areas, and relaxed way of life. Shaped by the Ayeyarwady River, the region features open views, small towns, and a strong connection to water and daily local life. Visitors can enjoy quiet beach trips, explore riverside communities, and experience a slower, more peaceful atmosphere. With its simple charm and easygoing feel, Ayeyarwady offers a refreshing escape from busier parts of the country.
