Discover the rich flavors and traditional dishes of Myanmar

Breakfast

Mohinga

The national dish, a savory fish-based broth with rice noodles, garlic, ginger, and lemongrass, served with crispy fritters.

Si Htamin

Glutinous rice cooked with turmeric, served with toasted sesame seeds and fried onions.

E Kyar Kway

Deep-fried breadsticks often dipped in hot tea or coffee for a perfect morning start.

Nan Gyi Thoke

A thick rice noodle salad mixed with chicken curry, fermented bean paste, and a squeeze of lime.

Appetizers & Snacks

Laphet Thoke (Tea Leaf Salad)

A famous fermented tea leaf salad with crunchy beans, nuts, fried garlic, and tomatoes.

Tofu Salad

Made from Shan-style yellow tofu (chickpea or split pea), served with garlic oil and chili.

Samosas & Fritters

Various fried vegetables or stuffed dough served with a tangy tamarind dipping sauce.

Mont Lin Mayar

Small, crispy rice flour cakes cooked with quail eggs, green onions, and chickpea in a special pan.

Bein Mont

A delightful Burmese pancake topped with poppy seeds, sweet coconut, and crushed peanuts.

Lunch & Dinner

Rice and Curry

A main, oily curry (fish, chicken, pork) paired with rice, seasonal vegetables, and spicy fermented fish paste.

Shan Noodles

Thin rice noodles with chicken or pork, served with savory garlic oil and pickled mustard greens.

Coconut Noodles

Also known as Ohn Noe Khao Swè, featuring noodles submerged in a creamy, rich chicken curry broth.

Kyay Oh

A comforting, savory noodle soup often featuring pork, meatballs, greens, and boiled eggs.

Desserts & Sweet Snacks

Sanwin Makin

A dense, baked coconut cake sprinkled with poppy seeds.

Shwe Yin Aye

A refreshing dessert with sago, agar jellies, and bread in sweet coconut milk.

Mont Let Saung

Pandan-flavored jelly noodles served in coconut milk and palm sugar syrup.

Kyauk Kyaw

A firm, multi-layered jelly dessert made from coconut milk and agar-agar.

Htamanè

A sticky rice dish with coconut, ginger, and peanuts, served during festivals.

Mont Lone Yay Paw

Glutinous rice balls filled with jaggery, topped with fresh coconut shavings.
